blueway

noun

blue·​way ˈblü-ˌwā 
plural blueways
: a route on a waterway designated for recreational use especially by nonmotorized watercraft (such as canoes and kayaks) and often for environmental protection
Currently, 350 miles of greenways and trails, as well as 170 miles of blueways, are open to the public within the Thread Trail system.—Latrice Williams
Additionally the trails, greenways and blueways would create recreational and environmental benefits.—Sharon Chanceis
A blueway trail is a small boat and paddling route that combines recreation, tourism, and environmental awareness along a water body.—The Journal (Ogdensburg, New York)
City leaders are moving forward with plans to develop a blueway system in the city's waterways, to give kayakers and other watersport recreationists safe access to the rivers.—Greg Wilkerson
—often used in the names of specific routes
