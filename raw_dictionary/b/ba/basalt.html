basalt

noun

ba·​salt bə-ˈsȯlt  ˈbā-ˌsȯlt 
: a dark gray to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite
basaltic
bə-ˈsȯl-tik 
adjective

Word History

Etymology
