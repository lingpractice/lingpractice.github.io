backassward

adjective

back·​ass·​ward ˈbak-ˈa-swərd 
variants or less commonly backasswards
ˈbak-ˈa-swərdz 
US, informal
: utterly or ridiculously backward, foolish, or wrong : ass-backward
… encouraging backassward thinking …—John C. Dvorak, PC Magazine, 9 June 1998
backassward adverb
or less commonly backasswards
We were doing everything backasswards that I thought we should be doing in the country. —Joseph Kennedy, quoted in Washington Post, 6 June 1993

Word History

Etymology

alteration of ass-backward entry 1

First Known Use

1971, in the meaning defined above
