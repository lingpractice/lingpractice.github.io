superflack

noun

su·​per·​flack ˌsü-pər-ˈflak 
plural superflacks
: an extremely skillful and successful publicity agent or flack
The more sophisticated War Department officials knew exactly where Jimmy [Stewart] would be most effective—as a superflack for the entire war effort.—Frank Sanello
They faithfully repeated Trump's claim that he didn't have a public relations agent, when in fact he employed one of New York's most prominent superflacks …—Howard Kurtz
