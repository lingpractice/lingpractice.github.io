hundo

noun

hun·​do ˈhən-(ˌ)dō 
plural hundos or hundo
US slang
: hundred
A new HDTV for only a couple hundo? That's a great deal.—Leah Stodart
Mascherin, a Florida Panthers second-round draft pick, finished the season with 35 goals and 65 assists. "It's a combination of everything," he said of hitting the hundo.—Josh Brown
Will Smith has confirmed via an extremely enthusiastic Instagram video posted overnight, that Bad Boys 3 is one hundo percent happening.—Mitch Feltscheer
The Amazon Tap is usually listed at $130, but it's on sale today for a cool hundo. That's $30 off, people.—Carlos Cadorniga
especially : a hundred dollar bill
For a crispy green one—or the swipe of a card if you aren't a gangster who deals purely in hundos—you will find yourself in possession of 100 soft tacos … —Serena Coady
… celebrity tipping stories that have superstars floating hundos down on their coffee tabs … —Kate Carraway
