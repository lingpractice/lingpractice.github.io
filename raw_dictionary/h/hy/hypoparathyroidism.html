hypoparathyroidism

noun

hy·​po·​para·​thy·​roid·​ism ˌhī-pō-ˌper-ə-ˈthī-ˌrȯi-ˌdi-zəm 
-ˌpa-rə-
: an endocrine disorder that is marked by a deficiency of parathyroid hormone resulting in hypocalcemia and that is characterized especially by muscle weakness and cramps, tingling or numbness of the lips, hands, or feet, and sometimes tetany

Medical Definition

hypoparathyroidism

noun
hy·​po·​para·​thy·​roid·​ism -ˌpar-ə-ˈthī-ˌrȯid-ˌiz-əm 
: an endocrine disorder that is marked by a deficiency of parathyroid hormone resulting in hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia and that is characterized especially by muscle weakness and cramps, tingling or numbness of the lips, hands, or feet, fatigue, and sometimes tetany compare pseudohypoparathyroidism
