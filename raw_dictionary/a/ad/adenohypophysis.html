adenohypophysis

noun

ad·​e·​no·​hy·​poph·​y·​sis ˌa-də-(ˌ)nō-hī-ˈpä-fə-səs 
plural adenohypophyses ˌa-də-(ˌ)nō-hī-ˈpä-fə-ˌsēz 
: the anterior glandular lobe of the pituitary gland
adenohypophyseal
ˌa-də-(ˌ)nō-(ˌ)hī-ˌpä-fə-ˈsē-əl 
adjective
or adenohypophysial
ˌa-də-(ˌ)nō-ˌhī-pə-ˈfi-zē-əl 

Word History

First Known Use

Medical Definition

adenohypophysis

noun
ad·​e·​no·​hy·​poph·​y·​sis -hī-ˈpäf-ə-səs 
plural adenohypophyses -ə-ˌsēz 
: the anterior part of the pituitary gland that is derived from the embryonic pharynx and is primarily glandular in nature

called also anterior lobe, anterior pituitary

compare neurohypophysis
adenohypophyseal
-(ˌ)hī-ˌpäf-ə-ˈsē-əl 
adjective
or adenohypophysial
-ˌhī-pə-ˈfiz-ē-əl
