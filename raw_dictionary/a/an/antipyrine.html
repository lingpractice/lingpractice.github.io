antipyrine

noun

an·​ti·​py·​rine ˌan-tē-ˈpī-ˌrēn 
ˌan-tī-
: an analgesic and antipyretic C11H12N2O formerly widely used but now largely replaced in oral use by less toxic substances (such as aspirin)

Word History
