antiprism

noun

an·​ti·​prism ¦an-ˌtī-¦pri-zəm 
¦an-tē-
plural antiprisms
: a polyhedron having two faces that are identical polygons in parallel planes but rotated from each other while the other faces are a series of alternately oriented triangles
These are the first clusters composed of centered square antiprisms of metal atoms.—Francis J. DiSalvo, Science, 9 Feb. 1990
antiprismatic
¦an-ˌtī-priz-¦ma-tik 
¦an-tē-
adjective
The ytterbium environment, in keeping with the diminished ionic radius, is now eight-coordinate with a square-antiprismatic arrangement. —Glen B. Deacon et al., Canadian Journal Of Chemistry, 1 Jan. 2009

Word History

Etymology

anti- entry 1 + prism

First Known Use

1969, in the meaning defined above
