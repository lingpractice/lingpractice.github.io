autofill

noun

au·​to·​fill ˈȯ-tō-ˌfil 
: a software feature that automatically enters previously stored information (such as a user's name or address) into a data field (as in a spreadsheet or on a web page)
She uses bookmarks and the autofill feature on her computer to enter an assortment of sweepstakes, some daily, some monthly, some just once.—Mary Hance
also : auto-complete
"The Dress" is still trending on Twitter and Facebook. I typed "the" into my Google search engine and the first autofill suggestion was "the dress." —Michelle Goodman
autofill transitive + intransitive verb
autofilled; autofilling; autofills
On the checkout page, it will alert you if a discount code is available for your purchase and autofill the code. —Gregory Karp
I discovered after googling it when I returned home: "Don't feed the" autofills with "trolls." —Carly Maga
