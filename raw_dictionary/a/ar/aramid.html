aramid

noun

ar·​a·​mid ˈa-rə-məd 
-ˌmid,
ˈer-ə-
: any of a group of lightweight but very strong heat-resistant synthetic aromatic polyamide materials that are fashioned into fibers, filaments, or sheets and used especially in textiles and plastics
