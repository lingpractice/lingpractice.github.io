am·​mo·​nite ˈa-mə-ˌnīt

Word History

Etymology

Noun (1)

New Latin ammonites, from Latin cornu Ammonis, literally, horn of Ammon

Noun (2)

Late Latin Ammonites, from Hebrew ʽAmmōn Ammon (son of Lot), descendant of Ammon

First Known Use

Noun (1)

1726, in the meaning defined above
