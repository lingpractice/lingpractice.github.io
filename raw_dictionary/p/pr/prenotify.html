prenotify

verb

pre·​no·​ti·​fy ˌprē-ˈnō-tə-ˌfī 
variants or pre-notify
prenotified or pre-notified; prenotifying or pre-notifying

transitive verb

: to inform or notify (someone) in advance
Passengers were prenotified that some flights might be canceled.
The commissioner must be pre-notified of proposed mergers with a transaction size of $87 million or higher.—Ian Graham
prenotification
ˌprē-ˌnō-tə-fə-ˈkā-shən 
noun
or pre-notification
plural prenotifications or pre-notifications
provided customers with written prenotification of the policy changes
