prepunch

verb

pre·​punch ˌprē-ˈpənch 
variants or pre-punch
prepunched or pre-punched; prepunching or pre-punching

transitive verb

: to perforate, cut, or emboss (something) in advance especially by means of a punch
Purchase one-inch-wide three-ring binders and prepunch the singers' scores before distributing them.—Arthur Wenk
You will need to prepunch holes for embroidering. Otherwise, it will be almost impossible to get your needle through the leather!—Kristen Sutcliffe
prepunched
ˌprē-ˈpəncht 
adjective
or pre-punched
prepunched paper/holes
