pyridoxine

noun

pyr·​i·​dox·​ine ˌpir-ə-ˈdäk-ˌsēn 
-sən
: a crystalline phenolic alcohol C8H11NO3 of the vitamin B6 group found especially in cereals and convertible in the organism into pyridoxal and pyridoxamine
