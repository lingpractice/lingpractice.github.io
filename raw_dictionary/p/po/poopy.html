poopy

adjective

ˈpü-pē 
poopier; poopiest
1
informal : filled with or covered in feces
Raising children forces adults into a life marked by spit-up stains, poopy diapers, homework assignments, …, hugs and kisses, circles under the eyes and, for those of us who work, bucketloads of guilt.—Amy Dickinson
A Ph.D. in microbiology is not necessary to grasp the concept: Clean water is good. Poopy water is bad.—Carl Hiaasen
2
informal : not good or pleasant : crappy
poopy weather
Though it never lost money, profits were "kind of poopy," says Johnny White.—Thomas A. Stewart

Word History

Etymology

poop entry 2 + -y entry 1

First Known Use

1967, in the meaning defined at sense 2
