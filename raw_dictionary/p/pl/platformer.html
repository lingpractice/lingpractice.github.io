platformer

noun

plat·​form·​er ˈplat-ˌfȯr-mər 
variants or less commonly platformer game
plural platformers also platformer games
: a video game where the play is primarily focused on moving past obstacles in real time with one's avatar by using various abilities (such as jumping and climbing) and items (such as weapons)
Mario games have traditionally been platformers, where the character has to run and jump, collect items and fight enemies.—Raju Mudhar
… Stray is not a challenging platformer, but will instead let players safely jump from platform to beam to air conditioning window unit to makeshift gondolas without fear of falling to one's death.—Michael McWhertor
It's a 2D platformer where you spray flames with water, use your axe to chop down obstacles, and even fly through the air with a jet of H2O.—Shaun Musgrave
She can walk and run and jump, like just about any other character from any other platformer game, but she is also capable of balletic dance moves.—Colin Campbell
