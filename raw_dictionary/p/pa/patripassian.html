patripassian

noun

pat·​ri·​pas·​si·​an
ˌpa‧trəˈpasēən,
ˌpā‧-
plural -s
often capitalized
: one who defends or advocates Patripassianism

Word History

Etymology

Late Latin patripassianus, from Latin patri- patr- + passus (past participle of pati to bear, undergo, suffer) + -ianus -ian
