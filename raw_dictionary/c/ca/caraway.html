caraway

noun

car·​a·​way ˈker-ə-ˌwā 
ˈka-rə-
1
: a biennial usually white-flowered aromatic Old World herb (Carum carvi) of the carrot family
2
: the pungent fruit of the caraway used in seasoning and medicine

called also caraway seed
