consequentialism

noun

con·​se·​quen·​tial·​ism ˌkän(t)-sə-ˈkwen(t)-shə-ˌli-zəm 
: the theory that the value and especially the moral value of an act should be judged by the value of its consequences
consequentialist
ˌkän(t)-sə-ˈkwen(t)-shə-ˌlist 
adjective or noun
