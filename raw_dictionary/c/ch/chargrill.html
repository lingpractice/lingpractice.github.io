chargrill

1 of 2

transitive verb

char·​grill ˈchär-ˌgril 
variants or char-grill
chargrilled or char-grilled; chargrilling or char-grilling; chargrills or char-grills
: to cook (food) on a charcoal grill
… a petit filet mignon, aged 30 days and chargrilled, served with a wild mushroom sauce.—Pamela Bieri, Desert Sun (Palm Springs, California), 20 Sept. 2013
… tables full of barbecued pork, char-grilled catfish, fried chicken, corn on the cob …—Jon Bowermaster, New York Times Magazine, 2 Dec. 1990
This cozy Northport pub hand-shapes a half pound of Black Angus beef before chargrilling it to a smoky juiciness.—Joan Reminick, Newsday (New York), 4 July 2013

chargrill

2 of 2

noun

char·​grill ˈchär-ˌgril 
plural chargrills
: a charcoal grill
Meat is cooked on a chargrill so any fat drips away …—Sarah Foy, Daily News (Plymouth, New Zealand), 22 Oct. 2013
