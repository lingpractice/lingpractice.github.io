cilium

noun

cil·​i·​um ˈsi-lē-əm 
plural cilia ˈsi-lē-ə 
1
: a minute short hairlike process often forming part of a fringe
especially : one on a cell that is capable of lashing movement and serves especially in free unicellular organisms to produce locomotion or in higher forms a current of fluid
2
: eyelash

Medical Definition

cilium

noun
cil·​i·​um ˈsil-ē-əm 
plural cilia -ē-ə 
1
: eyelash
2
: a minute, short, hairlike process that projects from the surface of most eukaryotic cells, is composed chiefly of microtubules, in unicellular organisms functions chiefly in locomotion, and in higher forms serves especially in the movement of fluids and particles (as the passage of mucus and dust from the lungs) and in sensory reception see ciliopathy
