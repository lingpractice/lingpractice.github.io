maskne

noun

mask·​ne ˈmask-nē 
: a breakout of acne or a related skin condition (such as dermatitis or folliculitis) that is caused by wearing a face mask (such as a surgical mask) over an extended period of time
Maskne occurs when people wear tight-fitting masks for long periods of time. A mask creates a moist environment from breathing, talking or sweating, which can block pores, leading to acne or other skin conditions …—Laura Lane
Simplifying your skincare routine can also help combat maskne. You should cleanse and moisturize your skin daily using a mild, fragrance-free cleaner and a light moisturizer with sunscreen …—Olivia Murray

Medical Definition

maskne

noun
mask·​ne ˈmask-nē 
: a breakout of acne or related skin conditions (such as dermatitis, eczema, or folliculitis) that is caused by wearing a face mask (such as a surgical mask) over an extended period of time
On facial skin, PPE [=personal protective equipment] creates a "dead space" between the face and the mask; this space traps sweat, oils, and dead skin cells beneath them that creates an ideal environment in terms of heat, humidity, and nourishment for the growth of bacteria. … Thus, typical maskne locations are areas covered by PPE such as the chin, nose and areas around it, cheeks, and areas around the mouth.—Izabela U. Malczynska et al., Dermatology and Therapy
The primary preventive measure for maskne is face washing in the morning and at the end of the day, using mild, non-soap cleansers that maintain the skin barrier.—Ayla Hadžavdić and Zrinka Bukvić Mokos, Acta Dermatovenerologica Croatica
