majoritarianism

noun

ma·​jor·​i·​tar·​i·​an·​ism mə-ˌjȯr-ə-ˈter-ē-ə-ˌni-zəm 
-ˌjär-
: the philosophy or practice according to which decisions of an organized group should be made by a numerical majority of its members
