misreckon

verb

mis·​reck·​on ˌmis-ˈre-kən 
misreckoned; misreckoning
Synonyms of misreckon

transitive + intransitive

: to reckon wrongly
… I realized with a pang of dismay that I had misreckoned the time while I lay in the utter darkness of my cell.—Edgar Rice Burroughs
: to make an incorrect estimate, judgment, or calculation
Yet in this sending of me to the Tower, these dogs who design it misreckon foully.—W. Outram Tristram
misreckoning noun
plural misreckonings
… I have learned to accept my responsibility and to forgive myself first, then to apologize to anyone injured by my misreckoning. —Maya Angelou
