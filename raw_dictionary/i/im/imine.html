imine

noun

im·​ine ˈi-ˌmēn 
: a compound containing the NH group or its substituted form NR that is derived from ammonia by replacement of two hydrogen atoms by a hydrocarbon group or other nonacid organic group

Word History

Etymology

International Scientific Vocabulary, alteration of amine

First Known Use

1883, in the meaning defined above
